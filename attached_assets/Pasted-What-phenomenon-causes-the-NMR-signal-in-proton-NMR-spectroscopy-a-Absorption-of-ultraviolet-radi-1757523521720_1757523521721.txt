What phenomenon causes the NMR signal in proton NMR spectroscopy?
a) Absorption of ultraviolet radiation
b) Absorption of infrared radiation
c) Absorption of radio waves
d) Emission of visible light
Correct: c) Absorption of radio waves

Why is tetramethylsilane (TMS) used as the standard in 1H NMR spectroscopy?
a) It reacts with most organic compounds
b) It has all equivalent protons giving one sharp peak at 0 ppm
c) It has multiple proton environments causing multiple peaks
d) It absorbs at the lowest frequency of all compounds
Correct: b) It has all equivalent protons giving one sharp peak at 0 ppm

What is the molecular formula of tetrachloromethane, a common NMR solvent?
a) CCl3H
b) CCl4
c) C2Cl4
d) Cl4
Correct: b) CCl4

Why are deuterated solvents like CDCl3 preferred over CHCl3 in proton NMR?
a) They have a higher boiling point
b) Deuterium nuclei do not produce interfering signals in 1H NMR
c) They react less with the sample
d) They contain more protons for easier analysis
Correct: b) Deuterium nuclei do not produce interfering signals in 1H NMR

In the low-resolution NMR spectrum of ethanol, the peak at about 1.2 ppm is caused by:
a) –OH hydrogen atoms
b) –CH2– hydrogen atoms
c) –CH3 hydrogen atoms
d) Aromatic hydrogen atoms
Correct: c) –CH3 hydrogen atoms

Which of the following ranges best corresponds to the chemical shift of aromatic protons in 1H NMR?
a) 0.9 – 1.7 ppm
b) 2.2 – 3.0 ppm
c) 3.2 – 4.0 ppm
d) 6.0 – 9.0 ppm
Correct: d) 6.0 – 9.0 ppm

According to the n + 1 rule, a proton having 3 neighboring protons will appear as:
a) A singlet
b) A doublet
c) A triplet
d) A quartet
Correct: d) A quartet

Why does the OH proton signal in the 1H NMR spectrum often appear as a singlet without splitting?
a) There are no neighboring protons
b) Rapid exchange of OH protons suppresses coupling
c) OH protons are not detected in NMR
d) OH protons always resonate at the same frequency
Correct: b) Rapid exchange of OH protons suppresses coupling

What effect does adding D2O to an NMR sample have on the OH proton signal?
a) It intensifies the OH peak
b) It shifts the OH peak downfield
c) It causes the OH peak to disappear
d) It splits the OH peak into multiple peaks
Correct: c) It causes the OH peak to disappear

In the high-resolution NMR spectrum of ethanol, why is the CH3 peak split into a triplet?
a) It has no neighboring protons
b) It has one neighboring proton
c) It has two neighboring protons on adjacent CH2
d) It has three neighboring protons
Correct: c) It has two neighboring protons on adjacent CH2

Which peak splitting pattern corresponds to a proton with 1 neighboring proton?
a) Singlet
b) Doublet
c) Triplet
d) Quartet
Correct: b) Doublet

Which of the following solvents would cause interference in a proton NMR spectrum?
a) CCl4 (tetrachloromethane)
b) CDCl3 (deuterated chloroform)
c) CHCl3 (chloroform)
d) D2O (deuterium oxide)
Correct: c) CHCl3 (chloroform)

Which of the following chemical shifts is most likely for aldehyde proton in 1H NMR?
a) 9.3 – 10.5 ppm
b) 0.9 – 1.7 ppm
c) 2.2 – 3.0 ppm
d) 3.2 – 4.0 ppm
Correct: a) 9.3 – 10.5 ppm

In the presence of how many adjacent 1H protons does a singlet splitting appear?
a) 1
b) 2
c) 3
d) 0
Correct: d) 0

How many signals will a symmetric molecule like benzene (C6H6) give in its 1H NMR spectrum?
a) 1
b) 3
c) 6
d) 12
Correct: a) 1

Which of these compounds will show a triplet and a quartet in its 1H NMR spectrum due to spin-spin splitting?
a) Methanol
b) Ethanol
c) Benzene
d) Propanone
Correct: b) Ethanol

Which number of hydrogen atoms corresponds to an NMR peak with an integrated area labeled as 2H?
a) One hydrogen atom
b) Two equivalent hydrogen atoms
c) Three hydrogen atoms
d) Four hydrogen atoms
Correct: b) Two equivalent hydrogen atoms

Why might the chemical shift for OH or NH protons vary depending on solvent and concentration?
a) Because of changes in molecular weight
b) Due to hydrogen bonding and exchange effects
c) Because OH and NH aren't detected in NMR
d) They do not vary; chemical shift is constant
Correct: b) Due to hydrogen bonding and exchange effects
